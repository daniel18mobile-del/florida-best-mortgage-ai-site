Florida homeowners who want to use home equity often compare a HELOC with a cash-out refinance. The right choice depends on your current mortgage, equity, goals, payment comfort, and how long you expect to keep the loan.

Neither option is automatically better. The numbers and purpose matter.

## When a HELOC May Be Worth Discussing

A HELOC may be useful when you want flexible access to equity and do not want to replace your current first mortgage. It may have a variable rate, draw period, and separate payment.

Ask about fees, rate changes, repayment terms, and whether the line amount fits your goal.

## When Cash-Out Refinance May Be Worth Discussing

A cash-out refinance replaces your current mortgage with a new loan. It may make sense if the new overall loan structure fits your rate, payment, term, and equity goals.
