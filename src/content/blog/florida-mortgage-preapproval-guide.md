Mortgage preapproval is one of the most important early steps in a Florida home search. It helps you understand your budget, gives your real estate agent a stronger starting point, and can help sellers see that your offer is backed by a real review.

Preapproval is not a guarantee of final loan approval. The property, appraisal, title, insurance, updated documents, underwriting conditions, and loan program rules all still matter.

## What You May Need

Most buyers should be ready to provide income documents, bank statements, identification, credit authorization, and information about debts or other real estate owned. Self-employed buyers may need business returns, profit and loss details, or additional documentation.

Maria and Florida Best Mortgage will tell you what is needed for your situation instead of giving you a one-size-fits-all checklist. Help is available in English and Spanish.

## What Preapproval Helps You Understand

A good preapproval conversation should explain estimated payment, cash to close, loan type, documentation needs, and any items to address before you make an offer.

In Florida, buyers should also review homeowners insurance, flood insurance, property taxes, condo or HOA details, and timing. These items can change the numbers even when the purchase price stays the same.

## Before You Make an Offer

Keep your finances steady while shopping. Large deposits, new credit accounts, job changes, or moving money between accounts may create extra documentation needs.

The goal is simple: know your numbers, understand the loan path, and move forward with a preapproval that matches your real situation.
