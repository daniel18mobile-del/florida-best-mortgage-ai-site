A reverse mortgage can be a planning tool for some Florida homeowners, but it should be reviewed carefully. The decision affects home equity, future plans, heirs, taxes, insurance, and long-term housing needs.

Dinerofacil LLC can help you understand basic questions and compare alternatives before you make a decision.

## What to Discuss First

Important topics include age eligibility, home equity, property type, primary-residence requirements, counseling, payoff of existing liens, available proceeds, and ongoing homeowner responsibilities.

You should also talk through alternatives such as refinance, HELOC, home equity loan, sale, downsizing, or family-supported planning.

## Florida Homeowner Considerations

Insurance, property taxes, condo rules, and maintenance costs are especially important in Florida. A reverse mortgage conversation should include the full cost of staying in the home.
