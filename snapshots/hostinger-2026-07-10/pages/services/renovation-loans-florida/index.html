Renovation financing can help when a Florida home has the right location or potential but needs repairs, updates, or improvements. Instead of using separate financing after closing, some programs may combine the mortgage and eligible renovation costs.

Maria can help you compare renovation options with construction loans, HELOCs, cash-out refinances, and standard purchase financing.

Common Renovation Scenarios

Borrowers may ask about renovation financing for kitchens, bathrooms, roofs, flooring, systems, accessibility improvements, storm-related updates, or repairs needed before a property can meet program standards.

Programs can vary. FHA 203(k), conventional renovation options, and refinance renovation structures each have different rules for contractors, draw schedules, inspections, and eligible work.

Review the Project Early

Before relying on renovation financing, review the scope of work, contractor availability, cost estimates, property condition, timeline, and appraisal expectations. The right loan should support the project, not create a surprise halfway through.

Can renovation financing help with a home that needs repairs?

Some renovation loan programs may allow eligible repairs or improvements to be financed with the purchase or refinance. Scope, contractor details, appraisal, property condition, and lender rules must be reviewed.
